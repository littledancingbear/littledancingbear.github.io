Indigenous Peoples

  • Definitions and terminology: Indigenous, Aboriginal, native
  • World population and location
  • Introduction of Western society and social infrastructure
  • Experiences with colonialism/imperialism
  • Cultural disintegration
  • Global dispossession



Generic value systems of Indigenous cultures

  • Reverence for and embracing of the land (Mother Earth)
  • Family and kin central to self, group and community identity
  • Holistic definition of health, healing and well being.



Perceptions of health: A cultural perspective

  • Indigenous
    • Holistic dimensions all encompassing: environmental, physical, emotional, spiritual and metaphysical
    • Gender relationships: access to and delivery of health services
    • Gender roles
    • Natural therapies.

  • Western
    • "Narrowly defined" and based on scientific knowledge - the "medical model"
    • Relationships between service provider and client
    • Technology
    • Medicines



Fundamental differences include:

Attitudes: Social marginalisation of Indigenous peoples promotes a sense of social, cultural, individual and group disharmony.

  • Promotion of ill health; behaviours that sustain ill health are
  • Social structure and relationships
  • Social Darwinism: classification and differentiation for various human groupings
  • Philosophical base for political strategy and policy

Indigenous cultures, where colonisation has occurred, demonstrate a congruency of negative patterns and results with respect to health and lifestyle. With acts of genocide and subsequent government policies in Australia, Indigenous Australians experience health status similar to Third World countries.


Legal Instrumentality

The legal position of many of the world's Indigenous people is somewhat precarious and this has produced detrimental outcomes with respect to their health status.

  • Police
  • Hospitals
  • Welfare agencies
  • Educational institutions



Comparison:

  • Nazis and Jewish peoples
  • South Africa and apartheid
  • America and Native Americans
  • Australia and Aborigines and Torres Strait Islanders



Legal Instruments in Australia

Policies of assimilation, segregation, integration, cultural adaptation and integration, self-determination and reconciliation have impacted on Indigenous health. Via these acts of legislation, Australia's Indigenous peoples were subjected to a multitude of inhumane experiences and became disempowered.
