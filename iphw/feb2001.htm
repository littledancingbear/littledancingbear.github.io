Archives: February 2001

Last updated: 1 March 2001






  • 27 February 2001:
    The World Bank has published a background note on "The International Development Goals: Strengthening Commitments and Measuring Progress", which is made available as a PDF-file (roughly 127KB) at:
    http://wbln0018.worldbank.org/news/pressrelease.nsf/673fa6c5a2d50a67852565e200692a79/4a919f9d02e7b391852569ff004629a1/$FILE/childpoverty.pdf

  • 26 February 2001:
    The International Narcotics Control Board, the independent and quasi-judicial control organ for the implementation of the United Nations drug conventions, has published its "Annual Report 2000", which is made available online as a series of PDF-files at:
    http://www.incb.org/e/ar/2000/index.htm

  • 24 February 2001:
    The background papers for the European Health Ministers' Conference on Young People and Alcohol have been made available online as a series of PDF-files at:
    http://www.youngalcohol.who.dk/English/documents.htm

  • 20 February 2001:
    The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change has published the report "Climate Change 2001: Impacts, Adaptation and Vulnerability". The summary for policymakers is available online as a PDF-file (roughly 144KB) at:
    http://www.usgcrp.gov/ipcc/wg2spm.pdf

  • 17 February 2001:
    UNFPA has published the report "Maternal Mortality Update 1998-1999", which is made available online at:
    http://www.unfpa.org/tpd/mmupdate/index.htm

  • 15 February 2001:
    The World Resources Institute together with the International Food Policy Research Institute have published the report "Pilot Analysis of Global Ecosystems: AGROECOSYSTEMS", which is available online as a selection of PDF-files (totalling roughly 1.5MB) at:
    http://www.ifpri.cgiar.org/pubs/books/page.htm

  • 13 February 2001:
    The Pan American Health Organization (PAHO), WHO's Regional Office for the Americas, has published the report "Nutritional Equity and National Food and Nutrition Security Programs: The Case Study of Jamaica", which is made available online as a PDF-file (roughly 247KB) at:
    http://newweb.www.paho.org/English/HDP/HDD/chan.pdf

  • 13 February 2001:
    The World Health Organization has published a document on "Practice and policies on user fees for immunization in developing countries", which is made available as a PDF-file (roughly 120KB) at:
    http://www.who.int/vaccines-documents/DocsPDF01/www564.pdf

  • 13 February 2001:
    The Natural Resources Defense Council, a USA based non-governmental organization concerned with environment and public health, has published the report "No Breathing in the Aisles: Diesel Exhaust Inside School Buses", which is made available online as a PDF-file (roughly 491KB) at:
    http://www.nrdc.org/air/transportation/schoolbus/schoolbus.pdf

  • 13 February 2001:
    UNICEF has published the first league table of child deaths by injury and concludes that injuries kill over 20,000 children aged 1 to 14 every year in the world's wealthiest nations. The new research, published in the UNICEF Innocenti Report Card, provides the most comprehensive estimates so far of child injury deaths across the member countries of the OECD. The report is available as a PDF-file (roughly 450KB) at:
    http://www.unicef-icdc.org/publications/pdf/repcard2e.pdf

  • 10 February 2001:
    The Ford Foundation has published the report "Worldwide workshop on youth involvement as a strategy for social, economic and democratic development", which is made available as a PDF-file (roughly 1.45MB) at:
    http://www.fordfound.org/publications/recent_articles/docs/youth.pdf

  • 9 February 2001:
    The Global Environment Facility, an international organization active in the field of renewable energy and global warming, has published the booklet "Renewable Energy: GEF Partners with Business for a Better World", which is available online as a PDF-file (roughly 71KB) at:
    http://www.gefweb.org/Whats_New/Postable_Renewable_Energy_Booklet.pdf

  • 2 February 2001:
    The International Labor Organization (ILO) has published the World Employment Report 2001. The overview plus the data sheets are made available online at:
    http://www.ilo.org/public/english/support/publ/wer/index2.htm
