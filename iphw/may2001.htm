Archives: May 2001

Last updated: 5 June 2001






  • 30 May 2001:
    Human Rights Watch has published the report "Scared at School: Sexual Violence Against Girls in South African Schools", which is made available online at:
    http://www.hrw.org/reports/2001/safrica/

  • 30 May 2001:
    The European Environment Agency has published the report "Environmental signals 2001: Environmental assessment report no. 8", which is made available online at:
    http://reports.eea.eu.int/signals-2001/index_html

  • 27 May 2001:
    Theresa M. Wizemann and Mary-Lou Pardue are the editors of the Institute of Medicine (USA) report "Exploring the Biological Contributions to Human Health: Does Sex Matter?" The book is made available online as a series of PDF-files at:
    http://www.nap.edu/books/0309072816/html/

  • 27 May 2001:
    EQUINET - The Network on Equity in Health in Southern Africa - is a network of research, civil society and health sector organisations, which offers access to its free newsletter and other publications

  • 27 May 2001:
    Sholom Glouberman, Director of Canadian Policy Research Networks, has published the report "Towards a new perspective on health policy", which is made available online as a PDF-file (roughly 530KB) at:
    http://www.cprn.org/docs/health/tnp_e.pdf

  • 26 May 2001:
    The International Labour Office (ILO) has published the report "Stopping Forced Labour", which is made available as a series of PDF-files at:
    http://www.ilo.org/public/english/standards/decl/publ/reports/report2.htm

  • 11 May 2001:
    The UN Economic Commission for Europe (UNECE) has published the report "Economic survey for Europe 2001", which contains comprehensive statistics for Eastern European countries. The report is available online as a series of PDF-files at:
    http://www.unece.org/ead/ead_h.htm

  • 10 May 2001:
    The WHO, Regional Office for Europe has published the second edition of its "Air quality guidelines for Europe", which is made available online as a PDF-file (roughly 900KB) at:
    http://www.who.dk/document/e71922.pdf

  • 10 May 2001:
    The European Commission has published the report "Economic Evaluation of Sectoral Emission Reduction Objectives for Climate Change", the objectives of which were "(i) to identify the (least-cost) contribution of different sectors and gases for meeting the Community’s quantitative reduction for greenhouse gases under the Kyoto protocol; and (ii) to determine a package of cost-effective policies and measures for all sectors and gases towards meeting the goals". The study, its data-sets and country-specific reports are available online at:
    http://europa.eu.int/comm/environment/enveco/climate_change/sectoral_objectives.htm

  • 10 May 2001:
    The Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) has published the report "OECD Environmental Outlook for the Chemicals Industry", which takes a close look into the environmental health and safety implications of the industry. The report is made available online as a PDF-file (roughly 1.2MB) at:
    http://www.oecd.org/ehs/ehsmono/RealfinalChemIndustryOutlookwithcovers.pdf
    This report follows the more general OECD report "OECD Environmental Outlook". Its highlights and some of the tables are made available online as a series of PDF-files at:
    http://www.oecd.org/env/outlook/outlook.htm

  • 9 May 2001:
    The UN Economic Commission for Africa has published the report "Transforming Africa's Economies", which is made available online as a series of PDF-files at:
    http://www.uneca.org/eca_resources/Publications/books/transforming_africas_economies/index.htm

  • 6 May 2001:
    The American Lung Association has published the report "State of the air 2001", which is made available online at:
    http://www.lungusa.org/air2001/index.html

  • 4 May 2001:
    The Population Reference Bureau has published the report "The World's Youth 2000", which is made available online as a series of PDF-files at:
    http://www.prb.org/pubs/world_youth_2000/

  • 3 May 2001:
    "A story to be shared: the successful fight against malaria in Vietnam" is the title of a report written by Dr Claudio Schuftan on behalf of the WHO Regional Office for the Western Pacific. The report is now made available online as a PDF-file (roughly 70KB) at:
    http://www.afronets.org/docs/malaria.pdf

  • 2 May 2001:
    The Regional Director of UNICEF for West and Central Africa, Dr Rima Salah, has written a report on "Child trafficking in West and Central Africa: An overview", which is made available online as a PDF-file (roughly 88KB) at:
    http://www.unicef.org/media/newsnotes/africchildtraffick.pdf

  • 1 May 2001:
    The World Bank has published the report "World Development Indicators 2001", which is made available online at:
    http://www.worldbank.org/data/wdi2001/
