An open letter to the members of
the Public Health Association of Australia

27 July 2000


We have tried to get this letter published in the Newsletter of the PHAA, InTouch, however we were told that there was no space available to publish the letter.

Thus we decided to publish it ourselves via e-mail, list-services and this web-page.

We leave it to you to draw conclusions regarding the performance of the Executive of PHAA.

Jan Ritchie, Eberhard Wenzel & Julie Woods

The Editor
In-Touch - Newsletter of the Public Health Association of Australia (PHAA)


Dear Editor:

as former SIG convenors we write to inform fellow PHAA members of our concerns over the manner in which the previous Executive Director, Lynne Flemming was dismissed and the reasons for us resigning from our positions as SIG convenors. We would like to express these concerns openly and to encourage members to consider the issues raised when voting for a new Executive and Council for PHAA.

First, we would like to publicly apologise to Lynne Flemming for the manner in which she was dismissed by the PHAA Council. We hold Lynne in high regard and believe her dismissal did not reflect her valuable contribution to the work, reputation and influence of PHAA. As SIG convenors, we have always had enormous support from Lynne and we cannot fathom the reasons for her very sudden termination.

Second, although two of us were present at the Council meeting that voted to dismiss Lynne (one as a voting member of Council), we are concerned that not all members present understood what was happening and what the implications were. Some Council members still do not have access to the details as to why she was dismissed.

Third, we are particularly concerned about the representation of SIGs on the Executive and the Council. The SIG convenors' representative did not convey to SIG convenors prior to the Council meeting the issues that were being raised and discussed about Lynne's position or the likely dismissal. As a consequence, we feel that the vote conducted at Council was not truly representative and that we were not able to participate in an informed manner. Currently all Branch presidents (7/7) have a vote at Council whereas only four (4/13) SIG convenors have a vote. We feel that this is inequitable and does not reflect the important and active role that SIGs play in the working of PHAA. The composition of voters at the April 2000 meeting may have been in accordance with the current constitution of PHAA and we cannot argue with this. However, we are concerned to ensure that this situation can not be repeated and we are therefore bringing this inequity to your attention. As SIG convenors we have had access to a consultant's report that describes options for governance of PHAA. From this a new governance review team has been appointed which we hope will ensure that the future governance of PHAA is more representative.

Overall, we are disappointed in the PHAA executive for handling the matter in the way they have. We have been informed that it was in accordance with the PHAA constitution and with Lynne's contract of appointment, but we are ashamed that an organisation that represents public health professionals who generally value consultation, transparency, honesty, conflict resolution and informed participation could have acted not only to stifle discussion of the reasons for Lynne's dismissal, but to effectively terminate a person's employment on the spot, with no opportunity for that person to respond and without going through a normal grievance procedure.

We made the decision to resign as SIG convenors because we were ashamed of the way this situation was handled, and because we no longer wanted to contribute to an organisation that would treat its employees so poorly.

Since the April Council meeting we have been part of a group of concerned members calling for greater transparency and information to members about the events leading to Lynne's dismissal. So far we have been unsuccesful in either arranging an extraordinary General Meeting to inform members or in having a letter signed by us all circulted via the PHAA listserve. We are currently hoping that members are made aware of the findings of the consultant's report before they are asked to vote for a new Executive and Council.

The Public Health Association of Australia needs to conduct its business in a way which is conducive to health for both staff and members if it is to have legitimacy in its influence on the health of the wider community. We do believe that workplace health promotion is a matter of quality management on all levels of any organization, particularly regarding the well-being of staff members.

Our decision to write this letter has been made with a deep commitment to public health in Australia. All three of us are saddened by what has happened, but we are also convinced that this sadness can be transferred to strength if we are able to analyse and respond appropriately to what has happened.

To be able to generate this strength, we need to be able to stand up and say what we want - in public.


Jan Ritchie, Ex-Convenor, Health Promotion SIG
Eberhard Wenzel, Ex-Convenor, International Health SIG
Julie Woods, Ex-Convenor, Food and Nutrition SIG
