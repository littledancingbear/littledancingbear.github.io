Fiji


Action taken Action proposed Issues Contact person
Participated in:
- Sydney workshop on school health promotion, Dec. 1994
- Fiji workshop for national coordinators of health-promoting schools, Oct. 1995

School health team activities (kindergarten-374; primary-700; secondary-147). These include inspection of school lunch, routine inspection on personal hygiene, taking height, weight, dental health services, toothbrush scheme, environmental sanitation, gardening, prizes during World Food Day

2 HPS underway since 1994 (Adi Cakobau School/Suva Grammar)

Since 1995 development of a third HPS in Vunisea Kadavu as part of healthy island initiatives

MOE set up an internal committee to coordinate the expansion of health-promoting schools

Workshop for facilitators in health-promoting schools, Dec. 1996. They will in turn train teachers to be facilitators within their education districts.

KANA/FSP Schools Meals project - which provides for the training of school personnel - cooks, managers, etc. - to attain and prepare healthy meals for boarders and any school in Fiji that is involved in the preparation of hot meals. Their aim is to ensure that nutritious, well-balanced meals are prepared for students.

BELS Programme is currently involved in improving the teaching and assessment skills of primary teachers, strengthening PIC sector planning and planning competence and building links with the community through the introduction on in-school life skills instruction and the promotion of greater parent-school interaction.

National Food & Nutrition Committee School Gardens Projects - which encourages the appreciation of good nutrition and healthy local foods.

Red Cross - is involved in the teaching of and provides training for Water Safety and First Aid. There is also a group of puppeteers who are Red Cross volunteers who accompany Health Team Visits and reinforce their Health Education talks with discussions on topical issues.

BEMTUP (Basic Education Management and Teacher Upgrading Project) is jointly funded and staffed by the Government of Fiji through the Ministry of Education and the Government of Australia through AusAID. A major part of the programme is the provision of an in-service programme for teachers of Years 7 & 8, and the revision of the English, Social Science, Mathematics and Basic Science curriculum. These syllabus are incorporating healthy principles and placing more emphasis on the teaching of life-skills rather than just the attainment of knowledge.

Start-up workshop for co-ordinators from 5 selected secondary schools in Suva (June 2, 1999).

Start-up workshop for co-ordinators from 5 selected primary schools in Labasa (June 4, 1999) 		Plan to use a new small community theatre group to work with the schools and village health workers on health promotion as part of this development

Long-term plan to develop all schools in Fiji into health-promoting schools (jointly by Ministry of Health and MOE)

By the end of 1997, to include 20 schools in the health-promoting schools programme 		Request for WHO support for training of teachers (partly to be covered through reprogramming within the Fiji country budget)

WHO-supported ASP 2000 Sportstart/Junior Sports programme (since 1995) in place: Viti Sport

Vanuatu


Action taken Action proposed Issues Contact person
Participated in:
- Sydney workshop on school health promotion, Dec. 1994
- Fiji workshop for national coordinators of health-promoting schools, Oct. 1995

Conducted 2 national cross-sectoral workshops in Sept. 95
Agreement by Health and Education Departments to begin health-promoting schools programme in early 1996
Designation of a focal person on health-promoting schools in DOH
Briefing on health-promoting schools during the national meeting of provincial education officers in August 1996.
National workshop on health-promoting schools: Towards healthy islands in Vanuatu, May 1997, resulting in a ni-Vanuatu version of the Regional guidelines for the development of health-promoting schools - A framework for action
Establishment of a national coordinating committee for health-promoting schools and appointment of a national coordinator in May 1997
Provision for health-promoting schools in the 1998-2000 country programme budget
Re-establishment of a HPS coordinating committee involving MoE, MoH and WHO in 1999. 		The Ministries of Education and Health will establish a national policy on health-promoting schools, which would ensure that health-promoting schools activities are initiated and implemented, and coordinating mechanisms are in place on national and provincial levels.
The Department of Education will take a lead role within the National Coordinating Committee on health-promoting schools, a well as on provincial and local levels, and closely collaborate with the Department of Health.
National Guidelines for the Development of Health-promoting Schools for Vanuatu will be issued on the basis of the suggestions made by participants in the First National Workshops on Health-promoting Schools, while reviewing the WHO Regional Guidelines (No. 5 in the WHO regional series on Health-promoting Schools).
The National Coordinating Committee will develop terms of reference for its activities and consider the incorporation of members from other relevant sectors.
Without any delay, schools will be identified in each of the six provinces and the two municipalities which would be willing to develop into health-promoting schools.
Training workshops will be carried out for the staff involved in health-promoting schools.
Resources will be identified to support improvements in the schools' physical environments on the basis of needs assessments.
School health education in the existing curriculum will be reviewed, improved and extended where necessary, and be made compulsory.
School health services will assure that they are covering all services, which are outlined in the Vanuatu Maternal and Child Health Manual (1995).
Health issues as entry points for the development of health-promoting schools, such as helminth infections or malaria, will be identified locally, and projects designed accordingly.
