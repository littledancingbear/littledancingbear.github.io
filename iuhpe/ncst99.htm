Fifth International Multidisciplinary Qualitative Health Research Conference

7 - 10 April, 1999, University of Newcastle, Faculty of Nursing, New South Wales (Australia)

Who the Conference is for?

Researchers and practitioners in medicine, nursing, sociology, medical anthropology, education, public health and social sciences.

Conference History

Conferences to date: 1991 Edmonton, Alberta, Canada; 1994 Hershey, Pennsylvania, USA; 1996 Bournemouth, England; 1998 Vancouver, BC, Canada and for the first time in the southern hemisphere, April 1999, Newcastle, Australia.

The Conference is usually attended by 500-600 delegates (from North America, Europe, and Australasia) and provides keynote papers from leading researchers in the field of qualitative studies; workshops on methodological, conceptual and ethical issues in qualitative research, and opportunities to engage in dialogue with international experts from a wide range of professional disciplines.

Keynote Speakers

  • Professor Benjamin Crabtree, University of Nebraska (medical anthropology)
  • Professor Lenore Manderson, University of Queensland (tropical health & anthropology)
  • Associate Professor William Miller, Lehigh Valley Hospital, Pennsylvania (family & community medicine)
  • Professor Janice Morse, University of Alberta, International Institute for Qualitative Methodology
  • Professor Margarete Sandelowski, University of North Carolina, School of Nursing
  • Professor Max van Manen, University of Alberta, Department of Secondary Education
