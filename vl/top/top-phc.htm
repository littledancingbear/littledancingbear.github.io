Topic's Information

 

  • Bureau of Primary Health Care (USA) - helps underserved and vulnerable people get the health care they need

  • Centre for Development and Innovation in Health (LaTrobe University, Australia) - "a unit of the Australian Institute of Primary Care at La Trobe university in Melbourne, Australia. CDIH has a focus on primary health care, community development and public health. CDIH operates as a research, consultancy, professional development, resource and policy analysis agency with a broad scope across the health system nationally"

  • Centre for Primary Health Care (University of Queensland, Australia)

  • Community Health Centers Around The World - a partnership of Management Sciences for Health and the National Association of Community Health Centers (NACHC), and funded by the United States Agency for International Development (USAID). In conjunction with the Electronic Resource Center (ERC) of MSH's Family Planning Management Development (FPMD) project, this Web site provides you with resources for learning more about community health around the world

  • Do Bugs Need Drugs - "a community education project to address the problem of antibiotic resistance. Antibiotic resistance has resulted from the overuse of antibiotics, mostly for respiratory tract infections. This website provides information for healthcare professionals, the public, teachers, parents and children"

  • Doctors for Global Health - "a private, not-for-profit organization promoting health, education and other human rights throughout the world. DGH currently focuses its efforts on providing primary health care and public health services to war-torn Morazan Province in northern El Salvador"

  • Creative Primary Health Care - Giving PHC a good name with tools and graphics and to creatively promote PHC

  • Culture Training Manual for Medical Workers in Aboriginal Communities (Australia)

  • Declaration of Alma Ata on Primary Health Care (1978)

  • Electronic Community of Primary Health Care Providers (Wisconsin, USA) - "We hope to use the tools of the Internet to build a cybernetic version of a community meeting place of value for today's time constrained and demand driven primary care providers"

  • First Aid Online

  • Health Care Information Resources - fine collection of resources provided by Tom Flemming (Canada)

  • HealthWrights - Workgroup for People's Health and Rights - "a non-profit organization committed to advancing the health, basic rights, social equality, and self-determination of disadvantaged persons and groups. We believe that health for all people is only possible in a global society where the guiding principles are sharing, mutual assistance, and respect for cultural and individual differences"

  • Management Sciences for Health (MSH) - "a private, nonprofit international organization working in public health areas to improve management and access to critical services in primary health care, child survival, maternal and child health, family planning and reproductive health"

  • Medical Technology & Practice Patterns Institute (MTPPI) (USA) - "is a nonprofit organization established in 1986 to conduct research on the clinical, economic, and social implications of new and emerging health care technologies. MTPPI's research is directed toward the formulation and implementation of local and national health care policies. In addition, MTPPI has been designated by the World Health Organization as a WHO Collaborating Center for Health Technology Assessment and in that role contributes to international health care research"

  • Primary Care Internet Guide

  • Primary Health Care in Mpumulanga (South Africa) - a detailed report on the situation in rural areas of South Africa

  • Primary Health Care Services in Malta

  • Project Concern International - "Project Concern International is a San Diego / USA based non-profit health organization, dedicated to saving the lives of children and mothers worldwide with basic medical care, nutritious food, clean water and health education. Stressing the education of local volunteers, we stretch the impact of donations and ensure that our programs will continue saving lives long after our work is done. More than 88 percent of Project Concern's cash donations go directly to support field programs

  • Topics in General Cross Cultural Health Care - a meta-site offering access some ethno-medical resources
