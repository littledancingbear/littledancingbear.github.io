        • National Centre for Classification in Health (Brisbane) - "to create an Australian centre of expertise in classifications for morbidity, mortality and health interventions and to develop Australian standards for the application of such classifications. NCCH's role in both morbidity and mortality classification will have a national scope. The focus of morbidity classification will be on inpatient and ambulatory services. NCCH is responsible for the development of ICD-10-AM, an Australian modification of the World Health Organizations' International Statistical Classification of Diseases and Health Related Problems - Tenth Revision (ICD-10) which is accompanied by an Australian procedure classification. ICD-10-AM is currently being prepared by NCCH in consultation with clinicians and clinical coders with the aim of more accurately reflecting Australian clinical practice. ICD-10-AM is due for implementation for morbidity coding in July 1998. The Australian Bureau of Statistics plans to use WHO's ICD-10 publication for cause of death coding"

    • Lead Education and Abatement Design (LEAD) - "a non-profit community group, set up in 1991 by parents of lead poisoned children and individuals who were appalled at the government's lack of services to address the needs of children affected by lead, and lack of a comprehensive strategy to prevent this most common environmental health problem. The LEAD Group's aims are to eliminate childhood lead poisoning in Australia by the year 2002, and to protect the environment from lead. "Childhood lead poisoning" is here defined (as it is by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention in the United States), as a foetus, infant or child having a blood lead level above 10 µg/dL (micrograms per decilitre). This equates to 0.48 µmol/L (micromoles per litre)"
