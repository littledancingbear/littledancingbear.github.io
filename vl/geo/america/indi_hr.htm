- America -
  • National Public Health Education and Research Facilities

    • American Indian Ethnobotany Database - Foods, drugs, dyes, and fibers of Native North American Peoples, provided by Dan Moerman, Professor of Anthropology, University of Michigan-Dearborn

    • American Indian Institute - located at the University of Oklahoma

    • American Indian Rehabilitation Research and Training Center (AIRRTC) - located at Northern Arizona University; its mission is to improve the quality of life for American Indian people who have disabilities by conducting research and training projects

    • American Indian Studies Center, Health Working Group - located at University of California, Los Angeles, the purpose of the Group is, "to mingle the expertise of community based American Indian organizations and American Indian people, with the resources of UCLA and its faculty, for the purposes of collecting data and developing culturally relevant programs that support healthy American Indian communities. The advisory panel constructs participatory/action research paradigms that include traditional/holistic definitions of health, i.e. social, spiritual, physical and mental well being"

    • American Indian and Alaskan Native Census Information Center (AIAN-CIC) - this site is under development

    • Center for American Indian and Alaskan Native Health - located at Johns Hopkins University, School of Public Health. "The Center operates nine satellite health stations on the Navajo, White Mountain Apache, and Gila River Indian Reservations and is engaged in projects with American Indian communities in South Dakota, North Carolina, Oklahoma, California, New Mexico and Alaska.
      The Center is the result of more than two decades of collaboration between the Johns Hopkins University and American Indian tribes. In 1991, it was formalized as an interdisciplinary academic center coordinated by the School's Departments of International Health, Maternal and Child Health, and Health Policy and Management. Upon the Center's founding, a Collaborative Agreement between Johns Hopkins University and the Indian Health Service was signed and a ceremonial blessing was performed by Chairman Ronnie Lupe of the White Mountain Apache Tribe, which has a 23-year history of collaboration with Hopkins.
      The objective of the Center is to work in partnership with tribes to provide strategies backed by sound research to improve the health and well-being of American Indians and Alaskan Natives. A chief priority is to train or educate more American Indian health leaders who will go on to direct the most culturally appropriate interventions for better health and safety among their peoples and bring greater self-sufficiency and self-determination to their tribes"

    • Center for American Indian Research and Education - "located at the University of California at Berkeley. The mission of CAIRE is to improve the status of Native people by promoting, developing, and evaluating culturally appropriate health, education, and social programs for American Indians and Alaska Natives nationally. CAIRE's priorities are: 1) the identification of barriers impeding optimal health care utilization, and 2) the development of culturally sensitive health and social service intervention models"

    • Flathead Head Start - implemented by the Confederated Salish & Kootenai Tribes: "At Head Start we emphasize the importance of early identification and treatment of health problems. Through the cooperative efforts of parents, staff, and health providers, each child receives the following:

      1. Physical Examination - including immunization update and hematocrit or hemoglobin.
      2. Vision Screening
      3. Hearing Screening
      4. Dental Examination
      5. Nutrition Services
      6. In addition, each child is weighed and measured three times during the school year. Mental health services are also available to children. Referrals for further evaluation and/or treatment of health problems are made as needed, and follow-up services are provided"

    • Fond du Lac Tribal and Community College: Human Services Program

    • Health Promotion Programs - located at the University of Oklahoma, "to meet the growing needs in the health promotion/wellness area for Native American people. Annually, HPP conducts the largest Native American Health conferences in North America--the annual Wellness and Women and Wellness and Native Men Conferences. Recognized as the leader in Native wellness and health promotion, HPP helps keep the circle strong"

    • Moose Jaw-Thunder Creek Health District (Canada) - "A healthy community in spirit, mind and body"

    • National Resource Center on Native American Aging - "serves the elderly Native American population of the United States. The center is committed to increasing awareness of issues affecting American Indian, Alaskan Native, and Native Hawaiian elders and to be a voice and advocate for their concerns. Through education, training, technical assistance, and research, the center assists in developing community-based solutions to improve the quality of life and delivery of related services to this aging population."

    • Native Elder Health Care Resource Center (NEHCRC) - a national resource center for older American Indians, Alaska Natives and Native Hawaiians, with special emphasis on culturally competent health care, located at University of Colorado Denver: Health Sciences Center

    • Native American Cancer Research Program - The purpose of the program is to develop and implement culturally relevant and acceptable cancer prevention and control research projects within local, regional and national Native American communities. The director of the program, Dr. Linda Burhansstipanov, is Western Cherokee and is the former program director of the Native American Cancer Control Program within the National Cancer Institute

    • Native American Diabetes Project (NADP) - developed by tribal governors and other leaders, tribal health workers (especially Community Health Representatives), interested community members (interviewers, artists), Indian Health Service professionals (doctors, nurse practitioners, physician's assistants, nurses, nutritionists, health educators, administrators, and others from the local service unit, area, and national levels), University of New Mexico professionals (physicians, nurses, health educators, exercise specialists, nutritionists, graphic artists, support staff)

    • Native American Health History - located at the University of New Mexico, "a collection of health related Articles and Literature concerning Native Americans. We currently possess copies of approximately 3,300 published articles within the years: 1764 - 1965. All of the articles have been indexed, assigned keywords, and abstracted to form a database of published Native American health information before the year 1966. Full text articles can not be viewed, but within the 'Search the Database' option we provide the Citation and an Abstract of any particular article"

    • Native American Research and Training Center - located at the University of Arizona

    • Nechi Training, Research and Health Promotion Institute - "Established in 1974 the Nechi Institute, now staffed by over thirty members is situated on 25 acres of land north of Edmonton. The location creates an ideal natural setting for individual growth and development. The 53,000 square foot building now the home of the Nechi Institute is shared with Poundmakers Lodge, a culturally based addictions treatment centre. Thus, becoming the only facility in Canada that combines an Aboriginal training centre with an Aboriginal treatment centre. It is the longest operating facility of its kind in North America. The shared facility which was designed with input from Aboriginal Elders includes training rooms, a cafeteria, gymnasium, a large ceremonial room, three sweat lodges, and residential facilities for 44 training program participants and 54 treatment clients.
      Poundmakers Lodge is one of Canada’s foremost culturally based addictions treatment centres. In addition to year-round Aboriginal treatment programs, Poundmakers hosts its annual Poundmakers Lodge Powwow featuring drum groups from all over North America and over 600 competitive dancers. Held on the grounds of Nechi/Poundmakers, the three day event takes place in July and is attended by well over 5,000 people"

    • Oklahoma Native American Prevention Research Center

      • Center For American Indian Health Research

    • Path to Healing - "a special partnership of the Native American Educational Serivces (NAES) College, the W.K. Kellogg Foundation and Americans for Indian Opportunity. The purpose of the partnership is to assist the Kellogg Foundation in defining a major Native American health initiative"

    • University of British Columbia, Institute for Aboriginal Health

    • Visions Center of Innovations - "committed to developing and communicating a deeper understanding of Aboriginal population health"
